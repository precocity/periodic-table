If you fork, redistribute, deploy, embed, or otherwise publicly share this project or any derivative of it, you must:

1. **Retain the Precocity logo and attribution** exactly as it appears in the original project (e.g., in the README, application UI, "About" screen, or footer — wherever it originally appears), including in modified or derivative versions.

3. **Not present your fork or derivative as an official Precocity product**, or imply Precocity's endorsement, sponsorship, or affiliation, unless you have separate written permission from Precocity.

A simple, compliant attribution looks like this:

> "Powered by [Precocity logo] Precocity LLC — original project available at [repo URL]"

## What You May Do

- Fork, modify, and redistribute the underlying code under the terms of the Apache License 2.0.
- State factually that your project is "based on" or "forked from" Precocity's [project name], as long as this doesn't imply endorsement and the required attribution is retained.

## Why This Exists

Open-sourcing the code is meant to encourage reuse, contribution, and adaptation — not to give up Precocity's brand identity or let others imply endorsement they don't have. Separating the code license (Apache 2.0) from the trademark policy is standard practice among open-source projects with a brand to protect, and lets us keep the code genuinely open while still protecting how the Precocity name and logo are used.
